# Checklist - What You Need to Run the Workshop

## The Script

Before hosting this event, you'll need to lightly add detail to the script to reflect the realities of your coastal town. The more recognisable you make your town to your participants, with relevant context about places, people, the lives of other beings, the more immersive it will feel.

By doing this, you help your participants bring their own experiences, passions, and challenges - fostering relationships within the group that inspire and enable community action.

### TCT Version
Download the [Script Template](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1kCO2BPgBhP7qiu0s7_RcT9f5_v1QroJgAd2I2unVeYI/edit?tab=t.0).

### TCP Version
Before hosting this event, you'll need to lightly add detail to the script to help your participants' connect their priorities to their experience of coastal regions that they know. Adapting the script with context about global polluters, wildlife and existing campaigns for positive eco-social outcomes will make this feel more relevant for participants and help them immerse themselves in the drama.

Download the [Script Template](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1FvTdhbb8jVUGHaMD3FmOtxkDyLDEO-aGNU6g0Uh9dgo/edit?tab=t.0#heading=h.ry6p11ckggkc).

## Equipment, Props & Aids Checklist

### Facilitator Materials
- A copy of the script for each facilitator

### Stationery
- Post-it notes and thick pens
- Character sheets (one per participant)
- Biros for each participant
- Name labels (two per participant: one for real name, one for character name)

### Furniture and Scene Props
- Chairs for all participants and facilitators
- Timeline poster: displayed on a wall or large tableâ€”big enough for participants to gather around and add Post-its (printed approx. 840mm x 1680mm)
- 4 tables to be used during character development, labelled "child", "young adult", "mature adult", and "elder"
  - Character development sheets distributed evenly between them
  - Name labels for people to write their character names on
  - Pens
- Pebbles: One per participant (or more), palm-sized and pocket-friendly, arranged on a small table

### Refreshments
- Water, soft drinks, tea and coffee
- A plate of retro biscuits (e.g. Jammy Dodgers, Custard Creams)
- Some sea-themed snacks like seaweed popcorn (keep the wrapper for use as a prop in the opening scene)
